GambleFit

GambleFit is a game for mobile devices which challenges players to think about their daily physical activity level. Every night, players check-in to the application and guess how many minutes of activity they did that day. If they guess correctly, they can earn Acticoins, the virtual currency used in the game. Furthermore, players can wager their Acticoins by setting higher fitness goals for the upcoming day. Guess carefully, however, because an incorrect guess will cost precious Acticoins!

Why GambleFit?

Recent studies have shown that physical activity monitoring devices, such as the Fitbit, do not actually aid in long-term habit formation or activity increase. Therefore, new ideas must be brought to the table on how to increase user activity levels within the Quantified Self movement. Habit formation requires intentional thinking by the user as well as behavioral adaptation to sustain and maintain changes in lifestyle. This will empower individuals to not only track their activity levels but also work to improve them over time.

One of the methods being explored currently is the ‘gamification’ of activity tracking, where users have to think about their activity levels and therefore evaluate if they are exerting enough throughout the day in order to ‘win’ the game. This is the gap that GambleFit seeks to fill. By making the game interactive and rewarding, we hope that users will be motivated to develop long-term exercise habits.

Our hypothesis is that individuals who are exposed to the gaming strategy of approximating their activity levels will be more engaged in the intervention and remain more physically active as compared to a simple display of fitness information (e.g. Fitbit).

System Overview

At the end of the day, GambleFit players must check-in with the application and guess how many minutes of moderate to vigorous physical activity (MVPA) they engaged in on that day. After guessing, GambleFit will show them how many minutes of MVPA they actually performed. Depending on how well players guess, they will either win or lose Acticoins.

After guessing activity for the current day, players can bet against GambleFit on how active they will be on the following day. By increasing their fitness goal, users can stand to earn a larger amount of Acticoins.

Calculating MVPA

GambleFit uses Google Fit to measure the player's MVPA for the day. Throughout the day, Google Fit will keep track of physical activity data on the player's smartwatch and smartphone. This data includes values such as step count, calories expended, and heart rate at 5-minute time intervals. At check-in time, GambleFit will query Google Fit to obtain these values for the day. For each 5-minute interval, the application process the fitness data through a decision tree to determine if the time interval corresponds to MVPA or not. If it corresponds to MVPA, the time interval is added to the total number of minutes of MVPA for the day.

For a more in-depth explanation of our MVPA calculations, please refer to our paper.

Experimental Setup

Our experiment targets people transitioning from the action stage of the transtheoretical model to the maintenance stage. However, due to time limitations, our study was only conducted over the period of about two weeks. In our study, we had two people playing GambleFit and two people using a control application, which is an application that simply shows users their minutes of MVPA each day. Our goal was to compare MVPA data between the two groups as well as compare levels of engagement between GambleFit and the control application.
